About Us

Our Story

The Auroville Theatre Group has been performing in Auroville for about 30 years now. Many actors have come and gone: several have started their stage careers with the ATG. The theatre scene in Auroville being pretty fluid — actors work with more than one company, move around from show to show. One thing has remained constant: Jill Navarre, ATG artistic director.

Ever since the first performance of 'Waiting for Godot', Jill has directed the ATG in its performances of 'Legend of the Kaliveli Siddha', 'The Greedy Man', 'Nishtha, or the Strange Disappearance of Margaret Woodrow Wilson', 'Sacrifice', 'Milarepa' — to name a few. The scripts are chosen for their beauty of language and depth of expression.

We always ask: What does this story mean to us, here, now? The plays we choose carry relevance to the Auroville experience — its multicultural, international spirit — and connect to what Auroville is all about: an expression of the search for human unity, man's aspiration for progress, the evolution of consciousness, unity in diversity, our connection with the Divine.

"Theatre is about transformation. It is about finding yourself and touching the deepest part of yourself in order to offer it up to the Divine, to the audience. You are there for them — you are enacting this miracle of transformation for them. And how do we open our hearts to allow this transformation to take place? We use the strongest power that we know — the power of love."

What is this love? It is the love which can change you, open you, widen you, clean you, make you strong. It gives you a voice, two arms, two legs, a strong, flexible, responsive, energized, dynamic body and most of all, a heart. It opens your heart, yes, until you think it will break.

Every day we come and start again. And every day there is some progress, understanding, some laughter and even some tears. Because you cannot change without pain, and frustration, and resistance. Then the love has to be there, even stronger than the strongest resistance — because it leads to a discovery of one's psychic being, to a feeling of connectedness, of light and great, great joy. And we are here for that.

Our Founder & Director

Jill Navarre

Artistic Director · Playwright · Screenwriter · Director

Born in New Orleans and raised in Brooklyn, New York, Jill Navarre began writing at the age of nine, winning a New York City-wide competition for best essay. She wrote poetry for a decade, had numerous poems published in the U.S. and Canada, and co-founded a celebrated Toronto poetry coffee house, The Soft Cell.

While working on her Master's degree in English, a playwriting class changed everything. She went on to win directing awards at the Source Theatre Festival in Washington D.C. for 'Rivers of Blood', and the D.C. Arts Council Larry Neal Award for playwriting for 'The Cradle Will Fall'. She directed 22 plays in the U.S. and India, and spent three years as Assistant Box Office Manager at the Folger Theatre, watching Shakespeare performed every night.

India first called in 1987, when a backpacking journey led her to Auroville — an international community in Tamil Nadu dedicated to human unity. She returned in 1991, knew immediately she belonged, and has lived there ever since. She has been Artistic Director of the Auroville Theatre Group since 1992. In 2003, she received a Government of India Development Scheme grant for directing 'Milarepa'.

Jill is currently writing 'Ananda' (a new play) and 'Portrait of Anna' (a screenplay), and has completed the screenplay 'Sannyasi', adapted from Satprem's novel.
